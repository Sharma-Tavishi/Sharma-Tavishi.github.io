About

I am a Computer Science and Mathematics student at Arizona State University's Barrett Honors College, continuing to Carnegie Mellon University for a Master's in Artificial Intelligence Engineering. My work bridges research and engineering: I translate difficult problems in trustworthy and agentic AI into systems that are reliable, interpretable, and practical to deploy.

Recent work includes a runtime safety-verification framework for AI agents, published at an AAAI 2026 workshop; an interpretable flight-delay prediction system developed as my honors thesis; and a multi-domain reasoning agent deployed on a research cluster. I have also conducted machine-learning systems research with NASA JPL, completed software engineering internships, and taught engineering courses at ASU for over three years.
